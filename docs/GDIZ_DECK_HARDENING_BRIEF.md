# GDIZ Deck Hardening Brief

## Objective
Strengthen the executive deck for GDIZ, industrial partners, and investors.

## Slide 1 — Business Model
### Core revenue streams
- Hardware sale per node
- Setup and deployment fee per site
- Monthly SaaS subscription per site
- CRM / WhatsApp campaign module
- Optional maintenance and replacement contract
- White-label or enterprise customization

### Pricing logic
- Entry pilot: low-friction deployment fee
- Recurring revenue from dashboard, analytics, and CRM features
- Upsell via multi-site rollout and premium support

## Slide 2 — Pilot 5–10 Tables
### Pilot scope
- 1 hospitality site
- 5 to 10 active tables
- 1 staff screen
- 1 manager dashboard
- QR per table

### KPI tracked over pilot period
- Time to respond
- Time to resolve
- QR scans per table
- Scan-to-lead conversion
- WhatsApp CTA clicks
- Staff adoption and error rate

### Pilot success condition
- Faster service handling
- Lead capture validated
- Manager dashboard trusted
- Clear path to larger rollout

## Slide 3 — Industrialization Roadmap
### Stage 1
- Prototype validated in hospitality environment
- Refine physical enclosure and durability

### Stage 2
- Pilot rollout in one site
- Gather operational data and customer behavior

### Stage 3
- Pre-series batch for selected venues
- Standardize onboarding and installation

### Stage 4
- Multi-site deployment package
- Analytics and CRM scaling
- Regional hospitality and premium reception expansion

## Slide 4 — GDIZ Fit
### Why GDIZ
- Industrial positioning and credibility
- Demonstration value for smart service infrastructure
- Strong story at the intersection of hardware, SaaS, and African operational modernization
- Clear path from pilot to industrial product narrative

## Closing message
GDIZ Smart Service Node is not just a service accessory.
It is a physical-to-digital operating layer for hospitality performance, customer capture, and recurring revenue.
